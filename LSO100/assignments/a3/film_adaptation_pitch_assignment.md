# Week 6 Assignment: Film Adaptation Pitch

**Weight:** 10%

## Topic of the Week

Immigration, Multiculturalism, and Belonging

## Core Reading

"Simple Recipes" by Madeleine Thien

## Assignment Overview

Stories often reach new audiences through film adaptations. For this assignment, you will imagine that you are pitching a film adaptation of Madeleine Thien's short story *Simple Recipes*. Your task is to explain how you would adapt the story for a contemporary audience while preserving its central themes and messages.

This assignment encourages you to think critically about how literary works are transformed across media and how stories about immigration, identity, family, and belonging continue to resonate in Canadian society.

## Instructions

Write a **500–700-word film adaptation pitch** that addresses the following:

### 1. Brief Overview

- Provide a short summary of the story.
- Identify the central themes you believe should remain at the heart of the film adaptation.

### 2. Your Vision for the Film

- Describe the setting, style, and tone of your adaptation.
- Explain whether you would keep the story in its original time period or update it for a contemporary audience.

### 3. Characters and Casting

- Discuss how you would portray the main characters.
- Explain how the characters help communicate ideas about immigration, multiculturalism, identity, family relationships, or belonging.

### 4. Key Scene

- Identify one scene from the story that must appear in the film.
- Explain why this scene is important and how you would present it visually.

### 5. Relevance to Contemporary Canada

- Explain why audiences today would find this story meaningful.
- Connect your discussion to at least one of the week's themes: immigration, multiculturalism, identity, belonging, cultural conflict, or inclusion.

## Requirements

- **Length:** 500–700 words
- **Format:** Paragraph form
- Include a title for your pitch
- Use examples from the story to support your ideas
- Submit through the Blackboard Submission Folder

## Learning Goal

By completing this assignment, you will explore how literary texts can be adapted into new forms while examining important Canadian themes such as immigration, multiculturalism, cultural identity, family dynamics, and the search for belonging.
