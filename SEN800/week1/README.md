## Computer security
- It refers to protecting and securing computers and their related data, networks, software, hardware from unauthorized access, misuse, theft, information losts, and other security issues. The internet has made our lives easier and has provided us with lots of advantages but it has also put our system's security at risk of being infected by a virus, of being hacked, information theft, damage to the system, and much more.

## Types of computer security
- Network Security:
    - Protects computer networks from unauthorized access, misuse, or theft.
    - Uses tools like firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and VPNs to monitor and control network traffic
    - Focuses on securing the infrastructure that connects devices and allows data to flow.

- Application Security:
    - Focuses on securing software and applications from cyber threats throughout their lifecycle.
    - Involves secure coding practices, regular vulnerability assessments, and the use of web application firewalls
    - Aims to prevent attacks that exploit vulnerabilities in applications.

- Information Security:
    - Ensures the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of sensitive data.
    - Uses the techniques like encryption, access controls, and data loss prevention to protect data at rest and in transit.

- Cloud Security:
- Endpoint Security:
    - Secures individual devices like computers, laptops, and smartphones from cyber threats.
    - Includes measures like antivirus software, endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools, and device managemment policies.
- IoT Security:
    - Focuses on securing interconnected devices and networks withtin the IoT ecosystem.
    - IoT devices are often overlooked but can be a major entry point for attackers.


## Threat Landscape
- Malware: Malicious software like viruses, worms, ransomware
- Phishing: Fake emails to steal login information
- Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks: Overloading systems to make them unavailable
- Inside threats: Employees misuing access.
- Zero-day attacks: Exploiting software vulnerabilities before they are patched.

## CIA Triad
### Confidentiality
- Ensure that information is accessible only to those authorized.

### Integrity
- Ensuring data is accurate and unaltered.

### Availability
- Ensuring authorized users can access data when needed.

## Symmetric vs Assymetric
- Symmetric Encryption
    - Uses the same key for encryption and decryption
    - Fast and efficient
    - Key distribution can be challenging
    - Example: AES (Advanced Encryption Standard)
- Asymmetric Encryption
    - Uses two keys: a public key (for encryption) and a private key (for decryption)
    - Solves key distribution problem
    - Slower than symmetric encryption

## Common Algorithms
- AES (Advanced Encryption Standard)
    - Symmetric encryption algorithm
    - Block cipher with 128, 192, or 256-bit keys
    - Widely used for secure data encryption in government and industry.
- RSA:
    - Asymmetric encryption algorithm
    - Based on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large prime numbers
    - Used for secure data transmission and digital signatures.
